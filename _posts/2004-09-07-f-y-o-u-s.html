Oracle makes a nice enough database, but the Oracle Ultra Search robot is a bandwidth pig that got on my nerves enough to block it. By precentage, it accounts for 10% of the traffic and 25% of the bandwidth for the *.fivesight.com web servers and typically gathers content with several parallel threads. You'd think it would be easy enough for the robot to support conditional GET, but since it doesn't, an HTTP 403 and exclusion in robots.txt are my version of FYO in response to the uninvited load test from Oracle's demonstration server. (For comparison, Google's crawler does support conditional GET for at least PDF files.)
